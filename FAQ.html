FAQ's


What is My Bhumi?

My Bhumi is “A Trust For Global Consciousness”, a learning organization that advocates and campaigns for sustainable development in India particularly in Karnataka. We are presently more than a hundred people around India working to end social lethargy and develop social vitality towards sustainable development.

As an organisation we do not believe in the concept of charity, we are all about creating opportunities and functioning as radical missionaries of Positive change. We lobby, we campaign to change human behaviour without punishment. We do that by evolving human through our engagements. We design our activities on reinforcing strategies and effective campaign based interventions, which are very personal, involving the mind, body and soul.

What makes My Bhumi different from the rest of them?

We define the behaviour of the problem by description and count.

We design our campaigns and activities as interventions and engagements that changes the behaviour of the problem.

We identify or construct effective reinforcers.

We apply the reinforcers through our engagements consistently to shape or change the behaviour

How is my Bhumi Governed?

My Bhumi is Governed by a Board of Trustees made up of individuals with extensive experience in advocacy, activism, policy making, politics, human resource, farming, education, engineering, psychology etc. The board oversees My Brumi’s work and ensures that My Bhumi is making progress on its mission. The full Board meets biannually, while the Board committees meet regularly and as needed. The committees include: Program, Executive, Development, Nominating and Membership, Audit and leadership team, under the direction of Founder Anish Kumar Sreedharan, guides and implements My Bhumi’s strategy.

How is My Bhumi Legally Structured?

My Bhumi as of today is a non-tax exempted organisation incorporated as a trust in Bengaluru district, Karnataka state. My Bhumi has several verticals under its belt, such as "Frillofarm"

How is My Bhumi Funded?

My Bhumi so far has been self-funded through a combination of activities, we do not solicit funds from the general public or from the government.
